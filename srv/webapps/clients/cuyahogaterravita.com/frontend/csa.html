What is a CSA?

Community Supported Agriculture (CSA) programs unite farmers and families around fresh local food. Farmers grow a variety of vegetables in a natural, sustainable way and pick them at the peak of flavor and nutrition. The community supports their local farmers by purchasing shares of the harvest and picking them up each week during the growing season. Together the farmers and CSA members share in the rewards and risks of farming.

What is unique about this CSA?

Located in the beautiful Cuyahoga Valley National Park, the Greenfield Berry Farm aims to provide fresh, local, and naturally grown produce to our CSA members. Unlike a traditional single-farm CSA, we can provide consistent quality, quantity, and a wider variety of produce by working with other local farmers who share our passion for small-scale farming.

Most of the produce will be from our farm, but we can get items from other local farms when we have a slow week or when they have something special that we don’t.

Even if something unexpected happens to our crops, our members will still get a share from the other farms that we work with.

What is the cost and what is included?

We offer 3 share sizes: HALF, FULL, and FAMILY. All memberships include berries (when in season) and a weekly newsletter with storage, prep, and cooking ideas along with seasonal recipes and updates from the farm.

Half Share

  • Pick up at the farm every other week
  • 18 pickups from January to November
  • $30 per pickup

Full Share

  • Pick up at the farm weekly
  • 36 pickups from January to November
  • $23 per pickup

Family Share

  • Pick up at the farm weekly
  • 36 pickups from January to November
  • $42 per pickup

What does my membership cover?

In addition to your weekly share of produce, your membership also covers: credit card fees; packaging; maintenance; and fuel costs to pick up items from our partner farmers when needed.

Share Sizes Vary With the Season
The quantity of produce in each share will vary with the seasons and weather conditions. The largest shares will be in summer when most produce is in season and the smallest in winter.

Vacation Policy

We have instituted a vacation policy for your convenience. Each family will be allowed 3 vacations per year. Families may choose to do one of the following before 10am on pickup day:

  • Donate: Request the share be packed and donated to a local foodbank and they will get the benefit of fresh locally grown food. Some of our partner farmers also donate to their communities, so your box may include items from them as well.
  • Give: Request that the share be packed and given to a friend that you send to pick up your box.
  • Double: Request that your next share be doubled up. Families with weekly shares will get 2x the boxes at the next pick up day. Families with bi-weekly shares may choose to get 2 boxes at their next scheduled pickup OR ask for a box the following week.

These options must be requested before 10am on the day of pickup. Otherwise the share cannot be packed or stored and we cannot offer a refund.
