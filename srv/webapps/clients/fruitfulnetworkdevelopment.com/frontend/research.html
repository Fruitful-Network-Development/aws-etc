Overview

Small Business Innovation Research (SBIR)

The Small Business Innovation Research (or SBIR) program is a U.S. government funding program, coordinated by the Small Business Administration, intended to help certain small businesses conduct research and development (R&D). Funding is provided through contracts or grants. The recipient projects must have commercialization potential and meet specific U.S. government R&D needs.

https://www.nifa.usda.gov/grants/programs/sbir-sttr/sbir-topic-areas

Readiness Roadmap

EIN, legal entity, banking, and grant portals

Foundation

EIN acquisition

  • Confirm business purpose, NAICS, and responsible party.
  • Capture IRS confirmation letter and store in compliance folder.
  • Use EIN consistently across banking, payroll, and grant portals.

Entity setup

Legal structure

  • Register LLC with state; collect Articles and Operating Agreement.
  • Document ownership to support SBIR/STTR affiliation checks.
  • Designate signatories for banking, SAM, and proposal uploads.

Cash management

Bank account

  • Open dedicated business checking tied to the EIN.
  • Configure dual controls for wires and grant disbursements.
  • Enable statements and 1099 reporting export for audit trails.

USDA 8.6

Rural and Community Development

National Program Leader: Carlos Ortiz, PhD

Commercialize tools that improve the quality of life in rural America.

  • Show the rural problem and how science-based tech addresses it.
  • Include climate and socio-economic impact objectives.
  • Balance high-risk concepts with practical deployments.

USDA 8.12

Small and Mid-Size Farms

National Program Leader: Dr. Rachel Seman-Varner

Enhance sustainability and profitability for small and mid-size farms.

  • Highlight dissemination to peer farms and local institutions.
  • Frame how the work supports rural economies and resource stewardship.
  • Quantify benefits for operations under $1M annual sales.

Proposal guidance

Aligning narratives to reviewer expectations

Structure

  • Open with the rural problem, quantified market gap, and the science-based solution.
  • Map work packages to commercialization milestones; call out partners and facilities.
  • List risks with mitigations and the go/no-go criteria for Phase II progression.

Clarity

Use plain, active voice. Support claims with citations, data tables, and graphics. Keep budget narratives specific to SBIR allowability while flagging any cost share.

SBIR/STTR qualifications

Eligibility notes for Fruitful Network Development

  • For SBIR, the small business must conduct >66% of Phase I R&D; STTR splits 40/30 between small business and research institution.
  • All key personnel must be primarily employed by the small business during the award period.
  • Confirm U.S. ownership and control; track any affiliate relationships early in SAM.
  • Document human subjects, animal care, or environmental approvals before kickoff.

Open source novel technology

How the Mycite Framework supports rural producers

FND's novel data technology is a system where local producers can digitally offer up their supply (in stock or expected) from the same data they manage their farm operations. This creates a shared data infrastructure that is just as seamless and efficient as what big agriculture uses in a top down model. This enables them to improve local economies, maintain a personal touch, and compete on product quality; without giving up any autonomy or control of their farming operations or their digital rights. There is a public demand for sustainably grown, local, healthy options and but the current system makes it financially and logistically hard to incorporate local producers. As a result, these farms under perform their industrial counterparts at about 10% per acre; just with respect to output of perishable crops that can't be planned for growth due to isolated suppliers, buyers and middle shops. Producers manage their website, inventory, POS, taxes, farm planning, and events through our desktop application. This aims to revolutionize the industry by creating the first refrigerated trucking and produce brokerage optimized for small agricultural producers at the same level as large industrial partners.
