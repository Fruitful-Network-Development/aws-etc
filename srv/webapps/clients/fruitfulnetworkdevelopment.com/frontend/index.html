For Agriculture

Do to a lack of use of small farmers, the US agricultural-industry looses out on approximately $70B/yr. This loss is just from what small farms could be producing, at lower waste, directly fed to local economies, and more sustainable.

Each year the US spends $1.1 trillion on food, but the hidden costs of poor diets, environmental damage and biodiversity loss add another $2.2 trillion, meaning the real cost of food is roughly twice what consumers pay.

Our goal is to develop the technology that anyone can employ for free, that flips the market incentive that currently incentivizes practices that degrade soil and ecosystems rather than rewarding sustainability.

For Producers

Our objective is enable the use of our small farms at the same financial and logistical convince as centralized-conglomerate farming.

Small farms make up 90% of the US farm land and simultaneously operate 10% less efficiency/per acre than industrial farms with respect to perishable crops, despite less waste. This is the result of farmers not being able to plan to grow what they can't sell.

The demand, the people, and the product are all here; it's the coordination that's broken.

For Consumers

We want to help a nation of people that feel ashamed of there ability to support themselves and to control what they eat. Local economies were a tragic casualty amidst enumerable benefits of globalism, by giving rise to centralized corporations to employ serve, and sustain.

We ate building the tools to transition, again, but back to a world where individuals aren't isolated; product and services aren't poor and inhuman; and people can afford to care because their lives and efforts can be devoted to the people around them.

Workware

In other words, we're building a system where local producers can digitally offer up their supply (in stock or expected) from the same data they manage their farm operations. This creates a shared data infrastructure that is just as seamless and efficient as what big agriculture uses in a top down model. This enables them to improve local economies, maintain a personal touch, and compete on product quality; without giving up any autonomy or control of their farming operations or their digital rights. There is a public demand for sustainably grown, local, healthy options and but the current system makes it financially and logistically hard to incorporate local producers. As a result, these farms under perform their industrial counterparts at about 10% per acre; just with respect to output of perishable crops that can't be planned for growth due to isolated suppliers, buyers and middle shops.
