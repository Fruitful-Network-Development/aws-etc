Agricultural Microstructure

Harnessing the insights of market microstructure to build a transparent, sustainable agricultural network that empowers small-scale producers, reduces waste, and unites local economies.

Why a Microstructure Approach to Agriculture?

Modern financial markets succeed because they make informationtransparent and continuously match supply and demand. Our Mycelial Exchange Model applies these same principles to agriculture, transforming fragmented, opaque supply chains into a real-time, semantic network of farms, markets, and consumers. By replacing centralized control with a shared language of data, we enable autonomous nodes (farms, cooperatives, and buyers) to coordinate efficiently without sacrificing independence.

The Mycite framework is the technological backbone of this transformation. Rather than creating yet another platform that locks farmers into a single system, Mycite provides a flexible semantic scaffold for interoperability, auditability, and local governance.

Our Mission for Phase I

Our company is pursuing a Phase I USDA SBIR research program to prototype and validate the Mycite framework in the field. We aim to prove that our semantic data fabric and matching engine can reduce agricultural waste, improve the financial resilience of small farms, and deliver fresh, sustainably grown food to local consumers.

Semantic Data Fabric

We build a unified vocabulary of entities (crops, farms, orders) and attributes (organic, location, quantity) using the Mycelium Schema Standardization. Farmers keep their own systems; Mycite automatically maps and reconciles differences so that everyone speaks the same semantic language.

Autonomous Coordination

Icon / image placeholder

Farms and cooperatives retain full control over their data and operations. They publish availability and subscribe to local demand signals; smart matching engines suggest exchanges based on geography, perishability, and sustainability attributes.

Economic Resilience

Icon / image placeholder

By pricing transport, perishability, and certifications into transactions, our system encourages buying local and rewards regenerative practices. This reduces waste, strengthens communities, and stabilizes income for small producers.

How the Mycite Framework Works

The Mycite network consists of several interoperable layers:

  • Identity & Access: decentralized identifiers and scoped API tokens protect sovereignty while allowing secure sharing.
  • Schema & Grammar: the MSS vocabulary establishes a shared grammar for all data, enabling each node to describe its own structures and understand others'.
  • Mapping & Diff: reversible mappings and semantic diffs reconcile differences across schemas without converting all data to one format.
  • Event Feeds: append-only feeds deliver updates (inventory, orders, logistic routes) to subscribers in near real time.
  • Policies & Provenance: machine-readable policies define sharing rules and certifications; built-in provenance traces ensure verifiability without centralized consensus.
